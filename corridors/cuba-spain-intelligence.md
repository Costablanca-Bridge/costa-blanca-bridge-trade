## ⚠️ Sanctions and Compliance Framework

Cuba operates under a distinct and complex sanctions environment:

### US Sanctions (OFAC — Cuba Sanctions Program)
- The US embargo (OFAC Cuba Sanctions) remains in force. This is the dominant compliance constraint.
- **Critical implication for CBB:** Any deal involving USD, US-person involvement, US-nexus financial institutions, or goods with US-origin content triggers OFAC jurisdiction — even for a Spanish firm operating in Spain.
- CBB must structure all Cuba-related activity to be **entirely non-USD and non-US-nexus**.
- Euro-denominated transactions through European banks with no US correspondent bank exposure is the standard approach.
- Goods with US-origin content >10% (de minimis threshold) require OFAC authorisation — check ECCN classifications carefully.

### EU Position on Cuba
- **No EU sanctions against Cuba.** The EU and Cuba operate under the **EU–Cuba Political Dialogue and Cooperation Agreement (PDCA)**, provisionally applied since November 2017.
- The PDCA creates a framework for trade, investment, and political engagement — it is the legal basis for EU commercial activity with Cuba.
- Spain implements no additional national restrictive measures beyond EU-level rules.

### Practical Compliance for CBB
- Avoid USD at every point in the deal chain.
- Avoid any counterparty or financial institution with US nexus on either side.
- All counterparties undergo standard KYC/AML — Cuban state entities (CIMEX, Habaguanex, Gaviota) require enhanced due diligence given state ownership and the opacity of Cuban state corporate structures.
- Clara (Legal) must review any deal structure before external communication.

**Status for CBB:** This corridor is viable for research and early-stage positioning. Compliance-clean deal structures exist — they require care, not avoidance.

## The Structural Opportunity for CBB

### Why CBB is Positioned for This Corridor

1. **The dollar bypass is native to our structure.** CBB operates in euros, through Spanish entities. We are not a US firm and have no US-nexus obligation. Structuring Cuba deals in euros through Spanish banks is how this corridor works — and it is exactly how we operate.

2. **The diaspora networks are in our geography.** The Galician and Asturian communities in northwest Spain — Vigo, A Coruña, Gijón, Oviedo — have commercial ties to Cuba that predate modern Cuba by a century. These are family businesses, import networks, and hospitality operators who know Cuba and want structured deal support. They are reachable.

3. **The trust architecture is relationship-first.** Cuban counterparties — whether private entrepreneurs (cuentapropistas) or state-adjacent entities — do not do business through cold outreach. They do business through introductions and demonstrated understanding. CBB's Cuban background (my personal history) is a credibility signal that opens doors that standard advisory firms cannot access.

## Three Lanes for CBB

### Lane 1 — Diaspora Commercial Bridge (Immediate, Low Risk)

**Profile:** Cuban diaspora entrepreneurs in Spain; Spanish businesses with existing Cuba exposure seeking structured commercial support.

- **What they need:** Corporate structuring for Spain-based operations with Cuba-linked revenue, banking access, cross-border payment solutions (non-USD), regulatory navigation for imports/exports.
- **CBB service fit:** LAND (Spanish entity formation), OPERATE (accounting, compliance, admin for Cuba-linked operations), TRADE Tier 1 (deal facilitation, counterparty introduction)
- **Regulatory risk:** Low — these are Spain-resident individuals and businesses operating under Spanish and EU law
- **Revenue model:** Service fees at Tier 1; success fees if we facilitate a specific deal or introduction

**First action:** Identify the Galician and Asturian business associations with Cuba connections. These are the entry points — chamber of commerce relationships in Vigo and A Coruña. Reachable through existing networks without cold outreach.

### Lane 2 — Cuban Premium Exports to Spain (Medium Term)

**Profile:** Cuban private-sector producers (rum, tobacco, seafood, cacao, biotech) seeking EU market access.

**Context:** Since 2021, Cuba has expanded the cuentapropista (private entrepreneur) and MIPYME (small/medium enterprise) legal framework. There is now a legitimate private sector in Cuba — small, restricted, but real — that is looking for EU market access outside the state export monopoly structure.

- **Target sectors:** Premium rum (non-Havana Club), artisanal tobacco, specialty seafood, Cuban biotech products (vaccines, pharmaceuticals under EU import licence)
- **CBB role:** Market access intelligence → distributor identification → import structuring → deal facilitation
- **Structure:** Tier 1 (intelligence and facilitation fee) with Tier 2 potential if CBB structures the first distribution agreement
- **Regulatory pathway:** EU food import requirements (SPS compliance), TARIC classification, non-US-origin certification
- **Counterparty risk:** Medium — private sector Cuban entities need KYC; state-adjacent requires enhanced DD

**First action:** Research which Cuban MIPYMEs are currently exporting to EU via third-country channels (Mexico, Panama, Spain directly). These are the credible counterparties already navigating the compliance environment.

### Lane 3 — Spanish Investment into Cuba (Long Term, Selective)

**Profile:** Spanish hospitality, real estate, and infrastructure investors with Cuba interest.

**Context:** Cuba's tourism sector (historically 4–5M visitors/year before COVID) is structurally underdeveloped and in need of capital. Spanish hotel groups (Meliá, Iberostar, Barceló) already have Cuba presence. The next tier — smaller Spanish family offices and hospitality investors — has Cuba interest but lacks the structure and local intelligence to act.

- **CBB role:** Deal intelligence, counterparty assessment, investment structure design (JV with Cuban state entity — the only legal form for foreign investment in most sectors)
- **Risk:** This is JV-with-state-entity territory. Cuban law requires majority Cuban ownership in most sectors. Deal structures are complex and require Clara's detailed review.
- **Position for CBB:** Monitor and position. Armando must approve before any investment-adjacent conversation.
