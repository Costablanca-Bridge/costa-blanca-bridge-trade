## Headline: Two Structural Shifts Worth Tracking This Week

---

### 1. EU–Mercosur Deal Approved — LatAm→EU Food & Agri Corridor Is Repriced

After 27 years of negotiation, EU member states voted in January 2026 to approve the EU–Mercosur free trade agreement. The deal is now pending European Parliament ratification.

**What this means for CBB:**

The agreement lowers trade barriers between the EU and Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, and Paraguay — four major food and agricultural exporters. The immediate effect is twofold:

- **Opportunity:** Brazilian and Argentine agri-food exporters will have a new, lower-friction pathway into EU markets. Counterparties in these markets who previously faced tariff barriers will be revisiting their EU entry strategies in 2026. This is a near-term origination window — companies that have product and track record but lack EU distribution, regulatory navigation, and capital structuring will be looking for deal partners.

- **Risk:** Increased competition for EU-domestic agriculture will generate political friction in some member states (France has already opposed the deal). Spain's agricultural sector may face pressure on specific categories. CBB needs to be sensitive to this context in any LatAm agri deal positioned to Spanish stakeholders.

**Corridor read:** LatAm→EU food and agri remains a priority corridor. The Mercosur deal accelerates the timeline on which this corridor becomes active. The counterparty profile worth pursuing: mid-sized Brazilian or Argentine producers with demonstrated export capacity seeking EU distribution or JV structuring.

**Immediate action:** This is worth a specific deal origination proposal for Q2. I will prepare a full origination memo for May's Monthly Council.

### 2. GCC Entering a "Resilience Phase" — MENA→EU Trade Bridges Are a Policy Priority

PwC's January 2026 GCC analysis confirms that Gulf governments are actively broadening their trade and investment relationships as a strategic response to softer oil revenues, geopolitical competition, and US–China friction.

The five themes defining the GCC in 2026: (1) broader trade bridges, (2) securing critical minerals supply chains, (3) AI adoption, (4) workforce transitions, (5) fiscal resilience.

**What this means for CBB:**

The GCC is in a deliberate diversification push. Governments and sovereign vehicles are looking to build non-hydrocarbon revenue streams and diversify trade relationships. This creates a structural pull for the kind of corridor structuring CBB does — connecting MENA capital and product with EU market access.

Specific sub-sectors relevant to CBB's MENA→EU mandate:
- **Food security and F&B:** GCC governments remain active buyers of food-related assets and supply chain positions given food import dependency.
- **Financial services and fintech:** Liberalisation in Saudi Arabia and the UAE continues to attract EU-based FS players looking for structured entry.
- **Technology hardware and logistics:** Critical minerals and supply chain security are creating new investment mandates from Gulf sovereign funds.

**Corridor read:** MENA→EU is the most active structural corridor in H1 2026. The GCC's focus on "broadening trade bridges" aligns directly with CBB's positioning. The counterparty profile worth pursuing: GCC-based family offices or mid-tier corporates seeking EU market entry or EU-based supply chain positions who do not have an established structuring partner.
